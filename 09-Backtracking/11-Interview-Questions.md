# Beginner

### 1. What is Backtracking?

Backtracking is a recursive algorithmic technique that explores all possible solutions by making a choice, exploring it, and undoing the choice if it doesn't lead to a valid solution.

---

### 2. What are the three main steps of Backtracking?

- Choose
- Explore
- Undo (Backtrack)

---

### 3. Why do we backtrack?

To remove the previous choice and try another possible solution.

---

### 4. What is Pruning?

Pruning skips branches that cannot lead to a valid solution.

---

### 5. Why is Backtracking recursive?

Because every decision creates smaller subproblems.

---

# Intermediate

### 6. What is a Decision Tree?

A tree representing every possible choice made during recursion.

---

### 7. What is State Space Search?

Exploring every valid state of a problem recursively.

---

### 8. Why is Backtracking better than Brute Force?

Because it avoids exploring unnecessary branches using pruning.

---

### 9. What is the worst-case Time Complexity?

Usually

```
O(2ⁿ)

or

O(n!)
```

depending on the problem.

---

### 10. Where is Backtracking commonly used?

- N-Queens
- Sudoku Solver
- Word Search
- Combination Sum
- Permutations
