# Beginner

### 1. What is a Queue?

A Queue is a linear data structure that follows the **First In, First Out (FIFO)** principle.

---

### 2. What are the basic Queue operations?

- Enqueue
- Dequeue
- Front (Peek)
- Rear
- isEmpty()
- Size()

---

### 3. What is the Time Complexity of Enqueue and Dequeue?

```
Enqueue : O(1)

Dequeue : O(1)
```

---

### 4. What is FIFO?

The first element inserted into the queue is the first one removed.

---

### 5. What are the applications of Queue?

- CPU Scheduling
- BFS Traversal
- Printer Queue
- Network Packet Processing
- Task Scheduling
- Streaming Systems

---

# Intermediate

### 6. What is a Circular Queue?

A queue where the rear connects back to the front, allowing efficient reuse of space.

---

### 7. What is a Deque?

A Double Ended Queue that allows insertion and deletion from both the front and rear.

---

### 8. What is a Priority Queue?

A queue where elements are removed based on priority instead of insertion order.

---

### 9. Why is ArrayDeque preferred in Java?

- Faster than LinkedList for queue operations
- Lower memory overhead
- Recommended for Queue and Deque implementations

---

### 10. What are common Queue interview patterns?

- BFS
- Circular Queue
- Deque
- Sliding Window
- Multi-Source BFS
- Priority Queue
