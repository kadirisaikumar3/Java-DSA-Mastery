# Google Interview Corner

Fenwick Trees are not usually asked directly as "Implement a Fenwick Tree."

Instead, interviewers present optimization problems where Binary Indexed Trees provide an efficient solution.

---

## What Google Tests

- Prefix Sum Optimization
- Efficient Update Strategies
- Bit Manipulation
- Problem Solving Ability
- Choosing the Right Data Structure

---

## Interview Tips

- Understand why `index & (-index)` works.
- Know the difference between Segment Tree and Fenwick Tree.
- Explain time complexity confidently.
- Write clean and readable code.
- Practice inversion count and range sum problems.

---

## Common Follow-Up Questions

- Why is Fenwick Tree faster than brute force?
- When should you use Segment Tree instead?
- Can Fenwick Tree support range updates?
- Why is it 1-indexed?
- Explain the role of the Least Significant Set Bit (LSB).

---

Strong conceptual understanding is often more valuable than memorizing the implementation.
