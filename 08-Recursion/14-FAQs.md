# ❓ Frequently Asked Questions

---

## Q1. Is recursion always slower than iteration?

Not always.

Some recursive solutions are more natural and easier to understand.

---

## Q2. Why does recursion use more memory?

Because each function call creates a new stack frame.

---

## Q3. What is StackOverflowError?

It occurs when recursion continues too deeply without reaching the base case.

---

## Q4. What is the difference between recursion and iteration?

| Recursion | Iteration |
|------------|-----------|
| Function calls itself | Uses loops |
| Uses Call Stack | Uses loop variables |
| Often simpler | Usually more memory-efficient |

---

## Q5. What is Tail Recursion?

A recursive function where the recursive call is the final operation.

---

## Q6. What are the most important recursion problems?

- Factorial
- Fibonacci
- Generate Parentheses
- Subsets
- Permutations
- Tower of Hanoi

---

## Q7. Biggest interview advice?

Don't memorize recursive code.

Understand how the problem becomes smaller after every recursive call.
